Dividing Polynomials

Dividing one polynomial by another involves a process similar to long division:

  1. Arrange Polynomials: Write the polynomials in standard form, with the dividend (the polynomial being divided) written under the division symbol and the divisor (the polynomial doing the dividing) on the left side.
  2. Divide: Begin the division process by dividing the first term of the dividend by the first term of the divisor. Write the result above the division symbol.
  3. Multiply: Multiply the entire divisor by the result obtained in step 2 and write the result below the dividend, lining up like terms.
  4. Subtract: Subtract the result obtained in step 3 from the corresponding terms of the dividend.
  5. Bring Down: Bring down the next term of the dividend and repeat steps 2 to 4 until there are no more terms to bring down or until the degree of the remainder is less than the degree of the divisor.

By following these steps, you can successfully divide one polynomial by another, obtaining a quotient and a remainder (if any).
