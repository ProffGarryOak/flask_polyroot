Finding Roots of a Polynomial

Finding the roots of a polynomial involves several methods depending on the degree of the polynomial:

  1. Quadratic Formula: For a quadratic polynomial (degree 2), you can use the quadratic formula to find its roots. The formula is given as:

    2. x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / (2a)

    where a, b, and c are the coefficients of the quadratic polynomial ax² + bx + c.

  2. Factorization: For polynomials of higher degree, you can use techniques such as factorization to find roots. This involves factoring the polynomial into linear factors and solving for the roots of each factor.
  3. Numerical Methods: If analytical methods are not feasible, numerical methods such as Newton's method or the bisection method can be used to approximate the roots of the polynomial.

By employing these methods, you can determine the roots of a polynomial, which are the values of x where the polynomial evaluates to zero.
